The Problems

Now that we know what is there, here is what those findings reveal about what is costing Factor RH leads right now.

Problem 1

The Funnel Only Works for the 3% Ready to Buy Today

The Factor RH website is built for the minority of visitors who arrive already decided. For the rest, which is everyone in research mode comparing options, evaluating timing, building internal cases, there is nothing to capture them, no resource to send home with them, and no sequence to stay in front of them until they are ready.

Here is what the buyer journey looks like today:

1

Discovery

An HR director or business owner searches for HR software in Paraguay, or gets referred to Factor RH by a contact.

2

Website Visit

They land on the site, navigate the modules, see the client logos. They are interested but not ready to commit a meeting slot to a vendor they just discovered.

3

No Middle Option

They look for something to take with them. There is nothing. The only option is a full demo request, which requires committing time and revealing themselves as a prospect.

4

Gone Without a Trace

They close the tab. Factor RH has no email, no way to follow up, and no knowledge they ever visited. That prospect continues evaluating and eventually chooses the vendor who stayed present.

Every week this continues, the Facebook Pixel and GA4 data accumulates traffic that never converts into anything.

Problem 2

No Nurture System Means Follow-Up Is Manual and Inconsistent

No email marketing platform was detected in the Factor RH site source. Every prospect who requests a demo and does not convert, every prospect who schedules and does not show up, and every prospect who asks questions on WhatsApp and goes quiet, falls into a manual follow-up process that depends entirely on the commercial team's bandwidth and timing.

No Automated Sequence After Demo Request

A prospect submits the form. They receive whatever the team sends manually. No structured email cadence delivering value, addressing common objections, or keeping Factor RH top of mind during the 45 to 90 day decision cycle.

No Recovery Sequence for No-Shows

When a prospect schedules a demo and does not appear, there is no automated outreach to reschedule, deliver value, or understand why. A warm lead that required effort to generate is abandoned by default.

No Follow-Up for Post-Demo Non-Buyers

A prospect attends the demo, is interested but not yet decided, and leaves. Without a structured sequence, Factor RH competes on recency instead of relationship. The vendor who follows up most consistently wins.

In a 45 to 90 day sales cycle, the company that stays systematically present throughout that window closes more deals than the one with the better product and inconsistent follow-up.

Problem 3

Eighteen Clients With Recognizable Names, Zero Case Studies Published

factorrh.com.py (no /blog, /resources, or case study section accessible)

Factor RH has deployed its platform at Nissan, UCP, Hilagro, Phytofarma, and Neotelecom, among others. These are recognizable brands across automotive, education, agriculture, pharma, and telecom. An HR director at any company in Paraguay who sees that list immediately asks the same question: what actually changed for them after implementing Factor RH? That answer does not exist anywhere on the site.

What Should Be There

One case study per anchor client documenting the before, the implementation, and the measurable outcome 90 days later. A 60-second video testimonial from the HR manager. A blog post adapted from the case study, distributed on LinkedIn, that reaches the next prospect before they ever visit the site. A PDF version delivered in the nurture sequence to prospects comparing vendors.

The client logos generate curiosity. The case studies close the credibility gap. Without them, the logos are decoration.

E Case Study Framework — Nissan and UCP

Factor RH has two clients that close the credibility conversation before it starts. Nissan and UCP. Neither story is being told. This framework builds both in the same session.

Block 1: Context
Company name, industry, number of employees managed, and what triggered the search for an HR software solution. Write this in their voice if possible.
Block 2: Situation Before Factor RH
Three specific problems in their own words. Include quantifiable friction: hours spent on payroll close, error rate in attendance records, time to onboard a new employee, documentation stored in paper or disconnected spreadsheets.
Block 3: Implementation
Which modules were activated first. How long the onboarding took. Who was involved on the client side. What the first 30 days looked like.
Block 4: Results at 90 Days
Two to three measurable outcomes. Format: "[X]% reduction in [metric]" or "[Y] hours recovered per [period]." Specific numbers beat general statements every time.
Block 5: Direct Quote
One sentence from the HR manager or director in their own words. What changed. How they would describe it to a peer at another company.
Distribution Plan
One-page PDF sent in the post-demo follow-up sequence. LinkedIn post with the headline result and link to PDF. 60-second video reel with the HR contact on camera. Homepage "Client Results" section replacing the static logo bar. Start with Nissan. That name does the work before the case study is even read.
